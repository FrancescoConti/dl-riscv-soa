RISC-V-based Deep Learning architectures

Methodology

For consistency, performance, power, and energy efficiency are evaluated in the nominal operating point: notice that this means usually the chart and table do not report the peak efficiency of the original publication, which in most cases uses a more efficient, lower-performance operating point. In at least one case (YUN), the nominal operating point is also not the highest-performance one, which is obtained by means of over-volting. Whenever one of the performance/power/efficiency triad is missing, it is derived from the other two. We consider 1 MAC = 2 OPs in all reported precision types. In case of multiple precisions reported, one or more representative ones were selected.

We distinguish between three "maturities": silicon, pre-silicon, and simulation. We report as silicon those prototypes or products for which our source (typically, an academic paper) reports measurements (performance, power, area) and typically a picture of the fabricated chip (e.g., Siracusa). We report as pre-silicon all architectures for which a detailed analysis of performance, power, and area has been performed through logic and physical synthesis (e.g., Archimedes). Finally, we consider simulation all architectures that are explicitly modeling a system (e.g., Bruschi et al.) as well as those for which our source does not report enough information to understand if logic and physical synthesis where performed.
